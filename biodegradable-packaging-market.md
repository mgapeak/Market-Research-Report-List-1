<p><p>The biodegradable packaging market is divided into two primary types: natural and synthetic. Natural biodegradable packaging is derived from renewable sources like plant materials, such as cornstarch, sugarcane, or wheat, which decompose easily in the environment. Synthetic biodegradable packaging, on the other hand, is made from polymers that are engineered to break down over time in the presence of specific conditions, such as moisture and temperature. Both types aim to reduce environmental impact and promote sustainable practices in packaging.</p></p>
