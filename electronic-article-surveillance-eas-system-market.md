<p><p>Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) systems are security solutions designed to prevent theft in retail environments. These systems typically include tags or labels attached to merchandise, detection antennas at store exits, and deactivation devices at checkout points. When a tagged item passes through the detection system without being deactivated, an alarm is triggered, alerting store personnel to potential theft.</p><p>The Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) System Market is on an upward trajectory, with a growth rate projected at a CAGR of 12% during the forecast period. Factors contributing to this robust growth include the increasing focus on retail security, the need to protect inventory, and advancements in EAS technology such as RFID integration, which enhances tracking and management capabilities. Additionally, the expansion of the retail sector, both physically and online, has led to increased demand for effective loss prevention solutions. </p><p>Latest trends in the market indicate a shift towards more integrated and intelligent systems that incorporate analytics and data processing to provide valuable insights. <p><p>The Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) market comprises several key components. Hard tags are durable, often made of plastic, used for high-value items. Soft tags, typically made from flexible materials, are suitable for clothing and can easily be removed at checkout. Deactivators or detachers are devices used to disable hard or soft tags after purchase. Detection systems are installed at exits to alert when items are stolen. Permanent deactivation tags are designed to remain inactive once deactivated, enhancing security for retailers.</p></p>
