<p><p>3D printing in the industrial market encompasses several technologies, each suited for specific applications. Stereolithography (SLA) uses UV light to cure resin, offering high precision. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) extrudes thermoplastic filaments, ideal for prototypes. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) uses lasers to fuse powdered materials, enabling complex geometries. Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS) fabricates metal parts with high strength. PolyJet Printing (MJP) jets resin for multi-materials. Electron Beam Melting (EBM) and Laser Metal Deposition (LMD) focus on metal components, while Direct Light Projection (DLP) enhances speed and resolution in resin printing.</p></p>
