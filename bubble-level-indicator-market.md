<p><p>Bubble level indicators are essential tools used to determine the level of a surface. The market for these indicators is divided into two main types: non-contact and contact types. Non-contact bubble levels use electronic sensors to assess alignment and do not require physical contact with the surface, enhancing accuracy and convenience. In contrast, contact types rely on traditional bubble vials, where a liquid-filled tube indicates level through gravitational force. Both types cater to different user needs across various industries.</p></p>
