<p><p>The general-purpose operational amplifier (op-amp) market consists primarily of bipolar and CMOS types. Bipolar op-amps are known for their high-speed performance and excellent linearity, making them suitable for analog signal processing applications. In contrast, CMOS op-amps offer lower power consumption, higher input impedance, and are well-suited for battery-operated devices. Both types cater to various applications, including audio, telecommunications, and industrial equipment, enabling engineers to select the ideal op-amp based on performance, power, and design requirements.</p></p>
