<p><p>The polymer gel market is categorized into two main types: ionic and non-ionic. Ionic polymer gels are formed when charged polymer chains interact with ions in a solution, leading to unique properties such as high swelling capacity and conductivity. They are commonly used in applications like drug delivery and tissue engineering. Non-ionic polymer gels, on the other hand, do not rely on ionic interactions and are characterized by their stability and biocompatibility, making them suitable for cosmetics, food, and biomedical applications.</p></p>
